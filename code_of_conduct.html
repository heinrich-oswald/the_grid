The Grid — Code of Conduct

The Grid is dedicated to providing a safe, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion (or lack thereof), or technology choices.

We expect all participants, including organizers, judges, sponsors, and attendees, to adhere to this Code of Conduct in all The Grid spaces—online and offline.

Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

Respect

Respect the boundaries, opinions, and contributions of others. Treat everyone with kindness and consideration.

Be Inclusive

Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities. Encourage participation from diverse perspectives.

Be Collaborative

Work together constructively. Share knowledge and lift each other up to create better solutions.

Be Professional

Maintain professionalism in all interactions. Focus on constructive feedback and growth.

Expected Behavior

  • Be respectful and considerate: Value each other's opinions, experiences, and contributions.
  • Be inclusive: Welcome new members and encourage diverse perspectives.
  • Be collaborative: Work together to solve problems and create something amazing.
  • Be patient: Remember that not everyone has the same level of experience or knowledge.
  • Be open to learning: Be willing to consider new ideas and approaches.
  • Give and accept constructive feedback: Focus on improving the community and each other.
  • Show empathy: Try to understand others' perspectives and experiences.

Unacceptable Behavior

  • Harassment: Offensive comments, verbal or written abuse, intimidation, stalking, following, unwanted photography or recording, sustained disruption of events, and unwelcome attention.
  • Discrimination: Racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior.
  • Sexual misconduct: Unwelcome sexual attention, advances, or behavior that creates a hostile environment.
  • Disrespectful behavior: Deliberate intimidation, trolling, flaming, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
  • Violence or threats: Threats of violence or actual violence, both physical and psychological.
  • Doxxing: Sharing private or personal information about others without their consent.
  • Plagiarism: Taking credit for others' work or ideas without proper attribution.
  • Cheating: Gaining unfair advantage in competitions through dishonest means.

Consequences of Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior from any community member, including sponsors, judges, and organizers, will not be tolerated.

  1. Verbal warning: A private, written warning from community leaders, with clarity about the violation and expectations for future behavior.
  2. Temporary suspension: Temporary suspension from all community spaces and activities.
  3. Permanent expulsion: Permanent expulsion from all community spaces and activities, with no possibility of appeal.
  4. Reporting to authorities: In cases of illegal behavior, we may report the incident to the appropriate authorities.

Reporting Guidelines

If you are subject to or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it immediately to the community organizers by emailing admin@thegrid.example.

When reporting, please include:

  • Your contact information.
  • Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved. If there are additional witnesses, please include them as well.
  • Your account of what occurred, and if you believe the incident is ongoing.
  • Any relevant contextual information.
  • If you have any evidence (screenshots, messages, etc.), please include them.

All reports will be handled with discretion. The safety and privacy of the reporter are our top priorities.

Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all The Grid spaces, including but not limited to:

  • All online platforms (Discord, forums, social media, etc.)
  • All in-person events and activities
  • All communication related to The Grid (emails, messages, etc.)
  • All events and challenges organized by The Grid
