Teams Challenge — Rules & Guidelines

Welcome to The Grid's Teams Challenge! This is a collaborative coding competition where teams of developers work together to create innovative projects. Below are the specific rules for this challenge.

Team Collaboration

Compete in teams of 2-3 members to build innovative applications.

Project-Based

Build complete applications with UI/UX, backend, and functionality.

Comprehensive Judging

Projects are evaluated on innovation, functionality, design, and presentation.

Eligibility

  • Age Requirement: Team members must be at most 17 years old and have parental consent to participate.
  • Experience Level: Open to all skill levels, from beginners to professionals.
  • Team Composition: Teams must consist of 2-3 members.
  • Single Entry: Each team is allowed only one entry per round of the challenge.

Team Structure

Team Size

Teams must have 2-3 members. No solo entries allowed.

Role Diversity

Teams should ideally have members with complementary skills (frontend, backend, design).

Team Captain

Each team must designate one member as the point of contact for communication.

Entry and Payment

  • Entry Fee: ₹200 per team (covers all members).
  • Payment Methods: UPI/Razorpay as announced in the Discord server.
  • Payment Proof: Save payment receipts — required for verification.
  • Slots & Scaling: Rounds start with 8 teams. Once a round fills, the next round's slots and prize double.
  • Refund Policy: Refunds will only be considered if requested at least 48 hours before the start of the challenge.

Timeline

  • Preparation Time: 7 days after registration closes for planning and setup.
  • Development Period: 48 hours of intensive coding and collaboration.
  • Submission Deadline: All projects must be submitted by the end of the 48-hour development period.
  • Judging Period: 2-3 days after submission for evaluation.
  • Results Announcement: Winners will be announced within 5 days of submission closing.

Submission Guidelines

  • GitHub Repository: Submit a link to your project's GitHub repository.
  • Demo Video: Provide a 3-minute video demonstrating your project's features and functionality.
  • Documentation: Include a README.md with setup instructions, features list, and team information.
  • Live Demo: If applicable, provide a link to a live demo of your application.
  • Original Work: All code must be written during the challenge period, though you may use existing libraries and frameworks.

Judging Criteria

The Teams Challenge is judged based on the following criteria:

30%
Innovation
30%
Functionality
20%
Design & UI/UX
20%
Presentation

Judges will evaluate each project holistically, considering both technical excellence and creative problem-solving.

Prizes and Payouts

  • Winners Selection: The top 3 teams with the highest scores will be declared winners.
  • Prize Distribution:
    • 1st Place: 50% of the prize pool
    • 2nd Place: 30% of the prize pool
    • 3rd Place: 20% of the prize pool
  • Payout Timeline: Winners will be paid within 48 hours after the announcement of results.
  • Payout Methods: Prizes will be paid via the method specified by the team captain during registration.
  • Prize Proof: Wallet transactions will be masked and screenshots posted in the Discord server and on the website.

Code Ownership and Usage

  • Intellectual Property: Teams retain ownership of their code, but grant The Grid a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, display, and modify their submissions for promotional and educational purposes.
  • Open Source: Teams are encouraged to make their code open source after the competition, but it is not required.

Fraud and Disqualification

  • Plagiarism: Using or copying code from others without proper attribution will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Cheating: Any form of cheating, including but not limited to using pre-written code, will result in disqualification.
  • Payment Fraud: Any attempt to manipulate the payment system will result in immediate disqualification and may lead to legal action.
  • Multiple Teams: Using multiple teams to gain an unfair advantage is strictly prohibited.

Important: These rules are specific to the Teams Challenge. Please also review our General Rules and Code of Conduct, which apply to all our challenges.

Stay Active: Make sure to stay active in the Discord server as all important announcements will be shared there.
