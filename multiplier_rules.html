Multiplier Challenge — Rules & Guidelines

Welcome to The Grid's Multiplier Challenge! This is a bi-weekly solo coding competition with scaling prize pools and participant numbers. Below are the specific rules for this challenge.

Bi-Weekly Competition

Held every 2 weeks with increasing prize pools and participant limits.

Scaling Format

Start with 10 participants and ₹500 prize pool, doubling each round.

Quarterly Reset

Every 3 months, reset to 10 participants to maintain competition quality.

Eligibility

  • Age Requirement: Participants must be at most 17 years old and have parental consent to participate.
  • Experience Level: Open to all skill levels, from beginners to professionals.
  • Single Entry: Each participant is allowed only one entry per round of the challenge.

Challenge Structure

  • Bi-Weekly Schedule: Competitions are held every 2 weeks.
  • Scaling Rounds: First round: 10 participants, ₹500 prize pool. Second round: 20 participants, ₹1000 prize pool. Third round: 40 participants, ₹2000 prize pool, and so on.
  • Continuous Participation: Participants can join subsequent rounds even if they participated in previous rounds.
  • Quarterly Reset: Every 3 months, the competition resets back to 10 participants to prevent overcrowding and maintain quality.
  • Round Format: A project prompt will be provided in the Discord server for participants to create.
  • Development Period: Participants will have one week to develop their project after the prompt is announced.
  • Language Choice: Participants can use any programming language they are comfortable with.

Entry and Payment

  • Entry Fee: ₹100 per participant per round.
  • Payment Methods: UPI/Razorpay as announced in the Discord server.
  • Payment Proof: Save payment receipts — required for verification.
  • Slots & Scaling: Rounds start with 10 slots and ₹500 prize pool. Each subsequent round doubles both the number of participants and the prize pool.
  • Refund Policy: Refunds will only be considered if requested at least 48 hours before the start of the challenge.

Submission Guidelines

  • Code Submission: Submit your code via the official submission form provided after registration.
  • Documentation: Include brief comments explaining your approach and any optimizations.
  • Testing: Ensure your code passes all test cases provided.
  • Deadline: All submissions must be received by the end of the 4-hour time period. Late submissions will not be accepted.

Judging Criteria

Prizes and Payouts

  • Winners Selection: The top 3 participants with the highest scores will be declared winners.
  • Prize Distribution:
    • 1st Place: 50% of the prize pool
    • 2nd Place: 30% of the prize pool
    • 3rd Place: 20% of the prize pool
  • Payout Timeline: Winners will be paid within 48 hours after the announcement of results.
  • Payout Methods: Prizes will be paid via the method specified by the winner during registration.
  • Prize Proof: Wallet transactions will be masked and screenshots posted in the Discord server and on the website.

Code Ownership and Usage

  • Intellectual Property: Participants retain ownership of their code, but grant The Grid a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, display, and modify their submissions for promotional and educational purposes.
  • Open Source: Participants are encouraged to make their code open source after the competition, but it is not required.

Fraud and Disqualification

  • Plagiarism: Using or copying code from others without proper attribution will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Cheating: Any form of cheating, including but not limited to using AI to solve problems, will result in disqualification.
  • Payment Fraud: Any attempt to manipulate the payment system will result in immediate disqualification and may lead to legal action.
  • Multiple Accounts: Using multiple accounts to gain an unfair advantage is strictly prohibited.

Important: These rules are specific to the Multiplier Challenge. Please also review our General Rules and Code of Conduct, which apply to all our challenges.

Stay Active: Make sure to stay active in the Discord server as all important announcements, including project prompts, will be shared there.
